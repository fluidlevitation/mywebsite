沖縄ファンダイビング

ダイビングライセンスをお持ちの場合、私たちが整理した楽しいダイビング旅行はあなたのためです！

With us you can do anything - from squeezing in a single dive during a short business trip to Okinawa, to dedicating an entire week to exploring in detail the many wonderful dive areas around the island. One or multiple dives per day, from early morning to night diving, we are flexible enough to accommodate any preference and schedule!

We can take you to various diving areas throughout Okinawa. The specific dive sites we can visit on a given day within each diving area depend on the sea conditions at that time as well as on your own preference, certification level, and experience. Some of our guests prefer to challenge themselves by venturing deep or exploring caverns and swim-throughs, while others feel more comfortable relaxing by the shallow colorful reefs. You set the rules!

We will take plenty of pictures and videos during your tour so that you can preserve your underwater memories for the years to come and share them with others. You will receive the files at no additional cost.

Boat, beach & night diving

ボートダイビングは、海を楽しむ最もリラックスした方法ですので、お勧めします and allows reaching the largest number of dive sites. The duration of each boat dive is limited to approximately 40-45 minutes for the sake of courtesy towards the other people riding the boat.

If you are a large group or would prefer not to share the boat with others, you can also charter the dive boats we use for private trips.

Those of you, who are up to the challenge of walking to the water with your gear on, can also choose shore diving. The duration of shore dives is only limited by your air consumption. However, shore diving is available only in the absence of waves and would limit the number of locations we could take you to.

If you are interested in observing the nocturnal marine life, we also offer night diving at selected dive sites.

Our equipment

自分の歯車を持っていない場合は、心配する必要はありません、あなたは私たちから必要なものすべてを借りることができます。Safety is our priority, so our rental gear is well maintained and regularly serviced.

We generally use 10L stainless steel tanks, however we also have access to a large selection of scuba tanks in order to accommodate different preferences, diver sizes, and air consumption rates. You can choose between 7L, 8L, and 10L aluminium tanks and 8L, 10L, 12L, and 14L stainless steel tanks.

We usually dive on air. However, if you have a nitrox certification, you can choose to dive on EAN32. Extra charges apply.

All our tanks are equipped with yoke valves (standard in Japan). If you have a DIN regulator, please bring a DIN-to-yoke converter, although we do have some spare converters you can use just in case.

Improving your diving

During and after fun dives we would often times give you tips and suggestion on how to improve your comfort and safety in the water. If you would like to improve your skills in a formal way, please consider signing up for our Advanced Open Water or Rescue Diver courses, or one of our many specialties. The two specialty courses we particularly recommend are Deep Diver and Nitrox.

If you have more than 100 logged dives under your belt, bring your diving to the next level with the Self-Reliant Specialty we offer. This course is a lot of fun and will open up your eyes to see the challenges and safety aspects of scuba diving from a completely new angle.

Getting around

Okinawa is relatively small, however you definitely do need a car to get around, especially when travelling to some of the dive areas located at the very north or very south of the island. This is why, for the convenience of our guests, we offer free pick-up service from hotels in Onna, Yomitan and Chatan. Pick-ups from other locations (including on occasion all the way from Naha airport) are also possible upon request. Please inquire.

We do think it is best that you take advantage of our pick-up service, especially since the travel time from your accommodation to the port can be used to get to know each other. After all, even though the main focus of scuba diving with us is the time spent in the beautiful Okinawan ocean, we do believe that personal interaction and interesting conversations can further improve your experience. Of course, you are also welcome to drive yourself to the port if you wish to do so.

Eating

If you choose an activity that requires us to spend the entire day on a boat, such as the Kerama Islands trip, we pick up lunch boxes from a convenience store on the way to the port in the morning. Otherwise, if the boat returns to port at lunch time, we usually eat at one of the nearby restaurants where you can sample the delicious Okinawan cuisine.

Dive boats in Okinawa

Unlike other countries in Southeast Asia, marine activities in Japan have historically been heavily regulated by the fishermen's union. This is why even to this day, almost all dive boats in Okinawa are controlled and operated by local fishermen.

When customers make a booking for boat diving with us, we in turn make a booking with the respective boat captain for the trip. This is how the vast majority of scuba diving shops in Okinawa operate.

This practice means that we do share the boats we use with other dive shops. Luckily, most dive operators in Okinawa are quite small, so the atmosphere on deck is amicable with enough space for everyone.

Once we enter the water and start the dive, we split from the other groups and try to find secluded areas so we can enjoy the calm marine environment in peace.

If you are a large group or would prefer not to share the boat with others, you can also charter the dive boats we use for private trips.

We have used the boat booking system on the island to our advantage and have built a large network of captains we work with who can take us to dive sites throughout Okinawa. This is particularly beneficial for guests who dive with us on multiple days, since it allows them to see how diverse scuba diving in Okinawa really is!

All the boats that we use are clean and reliable, and are staffed by captains with decades of experience, which makes our journeys safe and enjoyable. The vessels vary in size depending on how far we need to travel on them - from large cruisers with a sundeck and dry area, to smaller open deck boats. Entry in the water is done either through a giant stride or back roll.
