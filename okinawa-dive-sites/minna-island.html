Minna Island

Minna Island is a small crescent island in Okinawa located between the Motobu peninsula and Ie island, close to Churaumi Aquarium. The majority of dive sites here can accommodate both beginner and advanced divers. The topography of the dive area is sandy bottom, with the sand being so white that on sunny days it gives the water a characteristic blue color, which is truly spectacular. In addition, the corals at Minna Island are well preserved and are home to countless coral fish, rays, gardel eels and turtles. Nearby Minna Island is located Sesoko Island, the topography of which is very different but equally beautiful. The ocean floor at Sesoko is rocky with a number of crevasses and swim-throughs.

Details

Appropriate for: intermediate divers and snorkelers

Minimum number of participants: 2
Minimum number of dives per participant: 2

Boats leave daily at 9am and 1:30pm and each outing includes up to two dives. However, the schedule is flexible and special requests can sometimes be accommodated.

The surface interval is usually spent on the beach at Minna Island.

Departure is from the port in Motobu, south of Churaumi Aquarium. We offer free pick-up service from hotels in Onna, Chatan, Nago, and Motobu. Pick-ups from other locations are also possible upon request. Please inquire.

Boat travel time to the dive sites is ~20 min.

Toilet available at the port in Motobu and Minna Island.

We conduct many of our scuba diving courses in this dive area.
